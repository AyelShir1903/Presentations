Foundations · The Whole Rulebook
The ten principles of privacy
Canadian Federal privacy law (PIPEDA) is built on ten fair information principles.
1
Accountability
Someone owns privacy, and we answer for it.
2
Identifying Purposes
Say why we're collecting, up front.
3
Consent
Get meaningful agreement for each purpose.
4
Limiting Collection
Take only what the purpose needs.
5
Limiting Use, Disclosure & Retention
Use it only for that purpose; keep it only as long as needed.
6
Accuracy
Keep it correct and current.
7
Safeguards
Protect it in step with its sensitivity.
8
Openness
Make our practices easy to find.
9
Individual Access
People can see and correct their data.
10
Challenging Compliance
People can question how we handle it.
The four highlighted principles drive the two we lean on most, which is the next slide.

Foundations · Privacy by Design
Privacy risk can sneak in
The easiest fix is before a line of code is written. We know what privacy legislation expects of Echelon, so we can spot the risks.
Project kickoff note · tap the risky moves
We'll pull the full claims table into a spreadsheet so the vendor can test against real data, add a "just in case" contact field, and reuse last year's marketing list to seed outreach.
0 of 4 traps found
Data hoarding
Export the fields you need, not the whole table; that's the breach blast-radius.
Real data in test
Use masked data, and bind the vendor with a contract before anything moves.
Scope creep
A field added "just in case" is data you must protect forever.
Secondary use
A marketing list for a new purpose needs fresh consent, not a free ride.
PIAs
Privacy Impact Assessments
A structured way to find and reduce privacy risk before launch, the cheapest insurance we write.
?Let's look at these 3 scenarios together and you let us know if a PIA is Required.
1. Building a new claims-intake system that stores customer records.
Required. Law 25 mandates a PIA for any new system handling personal information.
2. Piloting an AI tool to summarize claims for adjusters.
Required. A novel, high-risk use of personal information, so a PIA comes first, not after launch.
3. Fixing a contact error on the Echelon webpage (no personal data involved).
Not Required. No personal information, no new use. Right-size the effort to the risk.
